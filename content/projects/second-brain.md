## Premise

The problem with most note-taking systems is that they optimise for input and neglect retrieval. A second brain should do the opposite: accept anything, and make everything findable. The ideal is a system that thinks with you, not just stores information.

## Approach

Three-layer architecture inspired by Andrej Karpathy's writing on knowledge systems. Layer one: raw sources, immutable, never edited after capture. Layer two: a wiki of synthesised entries, written and maintained by LLMs from the raw sources. Layer three: a context document (CLAUDE.md) that gives any LLM instant orientation.

## Outcome

A working knowledge system used daily, synced to GitHub, and connected to this portfolio via ISR. The most durable outcome is not the system itself but the discipline it enforces: everything worth knowing goes through one place.
