---
title: Philosophy & Slow Reading
caption: Stoicism, attention, craft
---

A return, in recent years, to the Stoics — Marcus Aurelius, Epictetus, Seneca — read alongside more contemporary writers on attention and craft: Pieper, Sennett, Berger, Ted Chiang.

The interest is practical, not academic. The questions that recur: _how to choose what to care about_, _how to remain useful without becoming busy_, _how to make objects that are honest about the time they took_.

The reading lives in margins, in a long-running commonplace book, and occasionally in essays here.
