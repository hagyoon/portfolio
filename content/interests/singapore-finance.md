---
title: Singapore Financial Architecture
caption: Local stack, global lens
---

The decisions a Singaporean makes in the first five years of working life compound for forty. CPF allocation, HDB timing, S-REIT exposure, MAS policy literacy — these are not minor settings on a generic personal finance template. They are the substrate of the actual financial system I live in.

Most investment writing is U.S.-centric. The Fed, the 401(k), Treasury yields, the S&P 500. Most of it is good. None of it is mine. The Special Account at 4% guaranteed, the zero capital gains regime, the S-REIT yield architecture, the MAS NEER policy framework — these are competitive advantages built into the Singaporean stack that no American playbook can capture.

The interest is practical, not patriotic. The country runs an unusually thoughtful financial architecture, and most people I know are leaving meaningful return on the table because they are applying a strategy designed for someone else's tax code to their own money. Writing about it here is partly to internalise the playbook for myself, partly to make the local stack legible to others who are trying to build wealth honestly inside it.
