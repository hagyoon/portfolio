A preference for cities you can read like a long sentence — Kyoto, Seoul, Lisbon, Mexico City — and for hotels that feel like rooms in someone&rsquo;s house rather than franchises.

Travel, here, is a research practice. Notebooks fill faster than camera rolls. Brutalist concrete and tea rooms get equal time. The point is not to see everything, but to come back changed in some small way that lasts.
