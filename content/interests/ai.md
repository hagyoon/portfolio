---
title: AI & Second Brain Systems
caption: Quiet infrastructure for thinking
---

I work with AI the way I work with watches — interested in the mechanism, sceptical of the marketing. The systems I build are deliberately modest: local-first where possible, plain markdown as the substrate, no dependence on a single vendor, no chat-first interface unless it earns its place.

The flagship is the second brain itself — a vault of raw sources, a wiki layer maintained by LLMs, and a schema document that gives any model instant orientation. It follows Karpathy's pattern: the LLM doesn't retrieve and regenerate every time; it incrementally builds and maintains a persistent artefact. Knowledge compounds. Cross-references are already there. Contradictions have already been flagged.

Around it sits an infrastructure of specialised agents — a manager that routes tasks via Telegram, specialists for research, code, market briefing, and writing. The architecture mirrors an org chart. Different bots, different employees. The system runs continuously on a Berlin VPS, handling work asynchronously while I sleep.

The thesis underneath all of it: we are early in a transition where the cost of building falls toward zero, and the scarce resources become taste, judgement, and the ability to specify clearly. The builder who can also think wins over the thinker who cannot build. Most of my time is spent making sure the systems I build amplify thinking rather than replacing it.
