I have come to the conclusion that the interesting question is not _what to collect_, but _how slowly_.

There is a version of collecting that operates like a portfolio: diversified, optimised, lightly held. There is another version that operates more like a long correspondence — one or two objects a year, each preceded by months of reading, each carrying with it the small history of its acquisition.

The first is a market. The second is a practice.

## A small test

When an object enters the second category, it changes the room around it. Other things look different. You begin to notice what you previously owned only by accident — the chairs you sat on without seeing, the watches you wore without winding properly, the books shelved out of order.

The test of a good acquisition, then, is not whether it appreciates. It is whether it _quietly improves the things already present_.

By that measure, most of my purchases of the last decade have been mistakes. The remaining handful are not.
